What’s Aging-in-place Remodeling?

As people age, they may face challenges with mobility, balance, accessibility, and safety in their homes. Aging-in-place remodeling refers to the modifications made to a home to make it more accommodating and accessible for seniors. These modifications can help seniors live independently and comfortably in their own homes for as long as possible. We’re excited to share some remodeling ideas that can help seniors live safely and comfortably in their own home as part of our aging-in-place remodeling services.

  1. Install grab bars and handrails Installing grab bars and handrails in strategic locations can greatly improve seniors' safety and mobility. Grab bars can be installed in the bathroom near the toilet and shower, and handrails can be installed on stairs and hallways. These features provide support, balance, and stability, making it easier for seniors to navigate their homes.

  2. Improve lighting Poor lighting can be hazardous for seniors, especially those with poor eyesight. Installing brighter and more energy-efficient lighting fixtures can improve visibility and reduce the risk of falls. With a little tech, we can install motion-activated lights in hallways, bathrooms, and other high-traffic areas.

  3. Consider a walk-in bathtub or shower A walk-in bathtub or shower can make bathing safer and more comfortable for seniors. These features eliminate the need to step over a high bathtub wall or climb into a shower stall, reducing the risk of falls. Additionally, adding a bench or seat inside the shower can provide a comfortable place to sit while showering.

  4. Replace doorknobs with lever handles Doorknobs can be difficult to operate for seniors with arthritis or limited hand mobility. Replacing doorknobs with lever handles can make opening and closing doors much easier. Lever handles can be operated with a gentle push or pull, requiring less effort and pressure than traditional doorknobs.

  5. Consider a stairlift or elevator Stairs can pose a challenge for seniors with mobility issues. Installing a stairlift or elevator can make it easier for seniors to move between floors of their home. Stairlifts are installed on the stairs and can carry the user up and down, while elevators are free-standing and can travel between floors.

Aging-in-place remodeling can greatly improve the safety and comfort of seniors in their own homes. These modifications can help seniors maintain their independence and quality of life for as long as possible.
